What is BIS Certification?

BIS Certification is an official product quality assurance granted by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), India’s national standards body. It ensures that a product conforms to the specific Indian Standard (IS) in terms of safety, performance, and reliability. The certification is especially crucial for consumer safety and is legally required for a wide range of products sold in India.

BIS Certification for domestic manufacturers falls under the ISI Mark Scheme (Scheme-I). Products bearing the ISI mark have passed stringent lab testing and factory inspections conducted by BIS authorities. It not only ensures regulatory compliance but also helps manufacturers gain customer trust and wider market access.

  • Mandatory Compliance for regulated products in India.
  • ISI Mark Labeling to assure quality and safety
  • Stronger Market Presence with better consumer trust
  • Eligibility for Government Tenders and large-scale supply chains

BIS Certification not only protects consumers but also supports manufacturers by increasing product credibility, brand value, and access to broader markets. Partnering with experts like SS Global Services can simplify the certification process and ensure complete compliance.

What is ISI Mark?

The ISI Mark is a certified symbol issued by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) that confirms a product's compliance with the Indian Standards (IS codes) for safety, performance, and quality. It stands for Indian Standards Institute, and is a mandatory certification mark for several products sold in India, especially in sectors like electronics, construction materials, kitchen appliances, and industrial goods.

Products bearing the ISI Mark have undergone BIS-approved lab testing and factory inspections, ensuring they meet the required safety and durability standards. For consumers, it’s a sign of trust. For manufacturers, it’s essential for legal sale and distribution in the Indian market.

  • Mandatory for over 150+ products as per BIS.
  • Ensures compliance with Indian safety and quality standards.
  • Authorized by BIS after successful testing and inspection.
  • Builds consumer trust and enhances brand reputation.

Without ISI Mark certification, manufacturing or selling regulated products in India is illegal. The ISI mark is not just a symbol—it's your gateway to regulatory compliance, market acceptance, and product credibility in India. Whether you're a domestic manufacturer or importer, securing the ISI Mark ensures your product meets the government’s required quality benchmarks.

Basic Requirements for ISI Certification

To obtain the ISI Mark, a manufacturer must comply with the essential criteria set by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS). These requirements ensure that the product meets the quality, safety, and performance standards defined under the relevant Indian Standard (IS code).

These criteria are mandatory for all domestic manufacturers who wish to legally produce and sell BIS-regulated products in India. Fulfilling these requirements is the first step toward getting BIS-certified and earning the trust of customers and regulatory bodies.

  • Manufacturing Unit in India.
  • Product must conform to the applicable IS code.
  • In-house testing lab with essential equipment.
  • Well-documented quality control system.

Fulfilling these basic requirements ensures that your product is eligible for BIS factory inspection, testing, and certification. Without these in place, the application may be delayed or rejected by BIS authorities. Meeting the standards not only ensures compliance but also strengthens your brand’s credibility in the Indian market.

ISI Mark Certification Process

The ISI Mark Certification process involves two methods: the Normal Procedure and the Simplified Procedure. Indian manufacturers may choose either route, while foreign manufacturers are required to follow the Simplified Procedure to obtain the ISI Mark License issued by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS).

Normal Procedure (60 to 65 Days)

  • Create a BIS login to start the application.
  • Set up in-house testing, ensure IS compliance, and gather documents.
  • BIS inspects the factory and verifies the process.
  • Sealed samples are taken for testing at BIS-approved labs.
  • If approved, the ISI Certificate is issued in 60–65 days.

Simplified Procedure (35 to 40 Days)

  • Register on the BIS portal to begin the process.
  • Prepare the lab, documents, and compliance details.
  • BIS visits the factory for inspection and sample collection.
  • Samples are tested at BIS-recognized labs.
  • If compliant, the certificate is granted in 35–40 days.

Documents for ISI Certification

Obtaining the ISI Mark Certificate from the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) requires manufacturers to submit a complete set of documents that demonstrate product compliance, factory readiness, and adherence to Indian Standards (IS codes). Proper documentation helps BIS verify the technical and legal eligibility of the manufacturing unit.

Below is a list of key documents required for a successful ISI Mark application:

Key Documents Required:

  • Company Incorporation Certificate
  • Address Proof of Manufacturing Unit
  • Factory Layout & Location Plan
  • Raw Material Details with Test Certificates
  • Flowchart of Manufacturing Process
  • Test Reports (In-house & BIS Lab)
  • Recent Bank Statement of Business
  • Product Drawings & Technical Specs
  • List of Manufacturing & Testing Equipment
  • Trademark Registration Certificate (if applicable)
  • Packaging Details (Size, Type, Quantity)
  • Proof of Factory Ownership or Lease Agreement

All documents must be clear, accurate, and up-to-date. Missing or incorrect documentation can lead to delays in the ISI certification process.
